### Avicenna’s argument for the existence of God- Logical Forms

Avicenna’s argument for the existence of God is known as the “Possibility-Necessity Argument” and can be formulated as follows:

1. Whatever exists contingently has a cause of its existence.
2. The universe exists contingently.
3. Therefore, the universe has a cause of its existence.
4. If the cause of the universe’s existence is itself contingent, then it too must have a cause of its existence.
5. This chain of causation cannot go on infinitely, and therefore, there must be a necessary being that is the ultimate cause of the existence of the universe and all contingent beings.
6. This necessary being is what we call God.

The argument can be explained as follows:

1. The first premise states that everything that exists contingently (meaning it doesn’t exist necessarily or by its own nature) must have a cause for its existence. In other words, anything that could have not existed, but does exist, must have a reason or explanation for why it exists.
2. The second premise asserts that the universe exists contingently. This means that the universe is not necessary and could have not existed. Its existence requires an explanation.
3. From premises 1 and 2, it follows that the universe has a cause of its existence. This cause is whatever brought the universe into existence.
4. However, if this cause is itself contingent, then it must also have a cause. This is because if something could have not existed, but does exist, then it requires an explanation for its existence.
5. This chain of causation cannot go on infinitely, because then there would be no ultimate explanation for why anything exists at all. Therefore, there must be a necessary being that is the ultimate cause of the existence of the universe and all contingent beings. This necessary being is not caused by anything else but is the cause of everything else.
6. This necessary being is what Avicenna identifies as God, the ultimate cause and explanation for the existence of the universe and all that is contingent.

In summary, Avicenna’s Possibility-Necessity Argument for God’s existence states that there must be a necessary being, which is the ultimate cause and explanation for the existence of all contingent beings in the universe, including the universe itself. This necessary being is what we call God.
